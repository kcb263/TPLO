🗓️ Recovery Timeline
Days 1–14 — Strict rest, short leash walks for bathroom only. Incision monitoring. Keep e-collar on.
Weeks 2–4 — Suture/staple removal (~14 days). Continue rest. Possible start of very limited passive range-of-motion exercises.
Weeks 4–8 — Gradual increase in controlled leash walks. Physical therapy exercises may begin. Follow-up X-ray.
Weeks 8–12 — Continued progression. Longer walks, gentle swimming may be allowed. Second follow-up X-ray.
Months 4–6 — Return to normal activity if healing confirmed. Full recovery typically 4–6 months.

🚨 When to Call Your Vet
⚠️  Excessive swelling, redness, or heat around incision
⚠️  Foul odor or discharge from the incision site
⚠️  Sutures/staples coming loose or incision opening
⚠️  Pain level 4–5 not managed by medication
⚠️  Refusal to eat or drink for more than 24 hours
⚠️  Vomiting or diarrhea lasting more than 12 hours
⚠️  Sudden worsening of lameness after improvement
⚠️  Pale gums, labored breathing, or collapse
